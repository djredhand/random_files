Building your personal brand

In today’s marketplace, it’s tough to stand out. Good grades and lots of extracurricular activities won’t guarantee that you’ll land the job or your dreams, or that you’ll even land the interview. There are lots of qualified candidates out there. The secret to standing out is to impress recruiters with the unique you—in person, on paper, and online. You need to create career marketing tools that will make the true you shine and leave them wanting more. It all starts with building your personal brand.

What is personal brand?

It’s your reputation. It’s about bringing who you are to what you do and how you do it. It’s about making your mark by being yourself—your best self. Think of your personal brand as your calling card—your unique promise of value. It is what you’re known for and how people perceive you.

  • What are your unique strengths, skills, and attributes?

  • How do you choose the career that’s right for you?

  • What do you want to be known for?

  • What will make you stand out in the eyes of potential employers?

  • Well…what are you waiting for?

PwC’s personal brand experience, Step Up to Stand Out, includes:

  • Steps to finding the right career path for you

  • Activities to discover your strengths, skills and unique talents

  • Keys to help you create a compelling story of you through:
    - Delivering a memorable elevator pitch
    - Crafting a winning cover letter, resume, and bio to capture the interest of recruiters

  • Guidelines to project a positive professional presence

  • Interviewing tips that will help you stand out among the competition

  • Strategies to build your network in person and online

  • Steps to developing professional online presence on LinkedIn

Q&A

  • Q: How did you gain so much responsibility so early as an associate?

    A: After working on several different projects during my first three months, I earned the trust of my directors and managers who gave me the opportunity to manage my own workstream. The directors and managers were clear about making sure I was able to handle the responsibility, and were there to support me throughout. I proved I was willing to go above and beyond my role as an associate. It was a rare opportunity, and it absolutely accelerated the growth of my career.

  • Q: Have your professional relationships influenced your career?

    A: My relationships have influenced my career immensely. I have always felt as if I had multiple people in my corner, people who I work with on engagements and those who are formally aligned to be my coach and relationship partner. Even when I first started, my coaches and senior associates went out of their way to introduce me to everybody in the office.

  • Q: What do you enjoy in your free time?

    A: I enjoy cooking healthy, playing intramural sports, volunteering, and exploring New York City.
